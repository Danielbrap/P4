Libero and DS


Overview of the Position

The position called Libero is a special player since they only play defense and cannot play offense. This position originated in the 1930s and is considered a speaker and is the team's best defensive player. A good rule of thumb is if the ball is close or a bit far away, let the libero get it since he will normally always pass better than you. The libero is the only position that isn’t able to serve due to rules and is the position that starts plays and makes the team fluid. The position called DS or defense specialist is the rarest position in volleyball since they replace an outside or an opposite player when they are playing defensive and they also only play defense. Since they’re technically replacing an outside or opposite they can serve and make the points from the backcourt.
